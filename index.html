About

Chiara began studying music at two years old. Since then she's studied under master musicians from all over the world.

Some of her musical accomplishments include playing in every level of the American Youth Philharmonic Orchestras, winning 1st place in the National Association Teachers of Singing (NATS) state competition and 2nd in the NATS regional competition, playing principal violist of the Washington Chamber Orchestra for several years, participated in the National Symphony Orchestra Fellowship Program, performed at the Sulzbach Rosenberg International Music Festival in Bavaria Germany, performed at various master classes throughout the world, attended the Jacobs School of Music Summer String Academy, made call-backs at Juilliard for voice, directed her choir "Philomena's Chorale" at The Worldwide Children's Holy Hour at the Basilica of the National Shrine of the Immaculate Conception in Washington D.C., modeled, acted, and sang for the agency AMTC, won a scholarship to attend the New York Film Academy (NYC), won 4 first place prizes in The American Fine Arts Festival at Carnegie Hall, and was offered the viola teacher position at The Menuhin Foundation of Bermuda.

She has received certification in Montessori from Ms. Elizabeth Calanchini at Renaissance Montessori School and Mrs. Maggie Radzik at Siena Academy. In addition to working at Montessori Schools, Chiara has experience working through the Reggio Emilia and Waldorf approach and in other private schools.

Chiara's private music studio, known as Philomena Studios, has always overflowed with joy and growth. Practicing, performing, and listening are the skills Chiara gives her students the opportunity to master. Her experiences with each student not only inspire her, but help to grow the unwavering dedication she has to the art of teaching music. Although she's currently on hiatus from teaching, she continues to give lessons to her three children and looks forward to the opportunity to grow her music studio again in the Maine Highlands!

Chiara and her family moved to the Maine Highlands in spring 2022 where they own 100 acres of forest, meadows, and a house they call "the cottage at Lindenhart". Home has become an incredible source of inspiration for her music and art. After all, from stillness and silence arise song!
